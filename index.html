Overshadowed By an ML Engineer

Internship

During my internship, I worked on a project called Florascope, aimed at using AI to identify diseases in potato plants. The idea for the project was born out of a science fair, where I initially developed a prototype system that could recognize plant diseases based on image data. Seeing the potential impact of this tool for farmers, I decided to expand on the project by collaborating with an experienced machine learning engineer to refine the model.

The primary goal of the project was to build a machine learning model capable of analyzing images of potato plants and detecting various diseases early, ensuring timely interventions. By improving disease identification, we aimed to reduce crop loss and improve yields for potato farmers.

  • Python
  • TensorFlow / Keras
  • Excel
  • Postman
  • The project began as an idea I developed for a science fair competition, where I presented a prototype.
  • I expanded on the project after the competition by collaborating with an experienced machine learning engineer.
  • Data Collection & Preprocessing: I gathered and cleaned large datasets consisting of potato plant images, both healthy and diseased. The data was essential for training and testing the machine learning models.
  • Feature Engineering: I collaborated with the ML engineer to extract relevant features from the images, focusing on patterns, textures, and discolorations that could indicate disease.
  • Model Training: I assisted in training convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to accurately classify diseases such as late blight, early blight, and other common infections in potato crops.
  • Collaboration: Though I often worked under the guidance of the more experienced ML engineer, I contributed to debugging and improving the model’s performance, helping to ensure that the algorithm could reliably detect diseases in diverse environmental conditions.
